What is the “A Gift of an Apple”?

The gift of an apple is a handbag that originates from apples. It's a family heirloom. In families, the handbag is cherished, used and mended and handed down to the next generation. Similarly to a branch of an apple tree which is always growing and adapting, the bag doesn’t have an end.

“A Greater Good”

Let us pass things down for the next generation. “The Flemish Orchard” maintains an environmental mission to reduce food waste. Our business model is sustainable. Our leather comes from apple waste. “The Flemish Orchard” turns food waste into something valuable and to be cherished. “The Flemish Orchard” believes that our products should be cared for and loved. If the bags tarnish, get broken or need a quick fix, we will lend a hand. We offer free repairs within the EU guarantee limit (2 year guarantee). If you return your product so we can recycle the apple leather or compost it, if its in poor quality, we will give you a voucher. The voucher will be 10% of product value.

All inclusive

Similarly to the diverse city of Paris, “The Flemish Orchard” aims for diverse employment. Diversity drives progress and innovation. Our policy is to ensure diverse policy, promote advancement of all types and groups of people. Diversity for “The Flemish Orchard” is not a quota or a public statement to the press. Instead our hiring process features management and HR staff being trained in recognising their personal bias and picking the more suitable candidate. “The Flemish Orchard” also offers training for graduate students with a minority background and/or low-income parents. Our company policy is to hire the best people, recognise their hidden talents and pursue action for their talent to grow. Everyone is welcomed to our family tree.

OUR STORY

The Flemish Orchard is the story of my family tree. My family branch stretches far. All the way from Ghent to Paris. The first part of my family branch is from France. My grandfather's side is from Paris. My grandfather, Pierre, dabbled in the arts and fell in love with my grandmother Elen. Elen came from the Flemish part of Belgium, Ghent, with the dream to find love in Paris. Her dream came true, when she met Pierre in the city of light. Together they discovered jazz, visited the French cultural scene and cherished life in the metropolitan city. The branches intertwined and my mother was born. Elen and Pierre told me to “never forget where you are from”. “The Gift of an Apple” embraces both the Parisian way of living and mixes it with the rich Flemish culture.
